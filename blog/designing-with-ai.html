Designing with AI: Where UX Meets Machine Learning

The conversation around AI in design often focuses on tools — which AI can generate layouts, write copy, or produce images. But the more interesting question is about the experiences themselves: how do we design interactions with AI that feel natural, trustworthy, and genuinely useful?

As interaction designers, we have a unique responsibility in this space. We're the ones shaping how people experience these powerful systems. And that means thinking beyond the interface to the underlying mental models, expectations, and emotional responses that AI-powered products create.

The Design Challenge of Uncertainty

Traditional interfaces are deterministic — press a button, get a predictable result. AI introduces probabilistic outcomes, and that fundamentally changes the design contract with users. How do you design for a system that might be wrong? How do you build trust when outputs vary?

The best AI experiences don't hide the machine — they create a transparent partnership between human judgment and computational capability.

What Interaction Designers Bring to the Table

Our training in user research, information architecture, and interaction patterns is exactly what AI products need. We know how to ask the right questions about user needs, how to structure complex information clearly, and how to design feedback loops that help people understand and control the systems they use.
